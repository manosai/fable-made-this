# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

AI builders, designers, researchers, and technically curious readers arriving from X or direct links. They are skimming for proof that Claude Fable produced unusually complete artifacts during its short public window, and they need the gallery to communicate that quickly without requiring deep context.

## Product Purpose

Fable Made This is a curated public gallery of media-backed Claude Fable artifacts. Its job is to make the strongest examples browsable, understandable, and impressive at a glance, while also preserving source attribution and synthesis about why Fable felt different.

Success means visitors can immediately see the artifact threshold: not isolated answers or benchmark claims, but complete worlds, engines, games, tools, agents, and polished product surfaces.

## Brand Personality

Selective, artifact-led, and slightly uncanny. The page should feel like an exhibition catalog for a brief capability window: confident enough to have a point of view, visual enough to reward browsing, and restrained enough that the artifacts remain the hero.

## Anti-references

- Text-only cards that require explanation before they look impressive.
- Bland AI landing pages with generic beige surfaces, repeated tiny uppercase labels, and uniform cards.
- Dark sci-fi dashboards or heavy model-launch hype pages.
- Benchmark-list pages where claims matter more than inspectable artifacts.
- Overly dense essay pages that bury the gallery under synthesis.

## Design Principles

1. Show, do not merely describe: every gallery card needs a visible artifact, screenshot, or video.
2. Average examples hurt the thesis: curation should prefer fewer stronger entries over a larger but weaker archive.
3. Make the artifact legible in under two seconds: category, creator, and why-it-matters should support the media, not compete with it.
4. Preserve source credibility: every claim should remain traceable to an original source or playable artifact.
5. Feel authored, not templated: the visual system should avoid saturated AI-page tropes and use layout, motion, and type with a clear point of view.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for contrast, focus states, keyboard navigation, and touch targets. Motion should respect reduced-motion preferences. The gallery should remain readable and navigable on mobile, tablet, and desktop.
